Financial Advice & Decision-Making Research

Strategic discovery & validation

Strategy

Context

In sophisticated financial environments, individuals balance a strong desire for autonomy with the need for reassurance at key decision moments. Experiences must support confidence without undermining control.

The Challenge

Understand when guidance meaningfully supports decision-making, when it introduces friction, and how digital experiences can adapt to different confidence and risk states.

My Role & Approach

I led end-to-end discovery and evaluative research across complex digital advice and investment journeys. This included moderated sessions spanning early consideration through active engagement, focusing on behavioural drivers such as confidence calibration, trust formation, and perceived risk.

The research examined how advice cues, language, and prompts influence decision momentum across different journey contexts.

Outcome & Impact

Insights informed experience strategy and prioritisation, helping teams introduce guidance where it strengthens decision confidence — and step back where autonomy is preferred.

Focus areas: Behavioural insight · Advice engagement · Moderated research · Complex decision UX

Digital Investing Journeys & Decision Confidence

Mental models, risk, and reassurance

Journeys

Context

Digital investing tools often assume rational behaviour, yet emotional confidence plays a decisive role in how people act.

The Challenge

Understand how users approach investing journeys depending on entry point, perceived risk, and confidence level.

My Role & Approach

I researched mental models around autonomy, guidance, and reassurance, comparing expectations and behaviours across different digital contexts and journey states.

Outcome & Impact

The research clarified when users want self-directed control versus guided support, informing differentiated design and content strategies.

Focus areas: Decision psychology · Journey research · Digital UX

Research Operations & Leadership Enablement

From research activity to decision-ready intelligence

Ops

Context

As research demand grows, senior leaders often lack visibility into how research aligns with strategy, timing, and investment.

The Challenge

Enable leadership to prioritise, resource, and act — without exposing sensitive project detail.

My Role & Approach

I designed an integrated research enablement framework combining strategic research roadmapping, capacity forecasting across research streams, and a leadership-facing dashboard translating research activity into executive signals.

All components were deliberately framed through a governance and decision-support lens.

Outcome & Impact

  • Improved leadership confidence in research planning and delivery
  • Clearer prioritisation and resourcing conversations
  • Reduced reactive and ad-hoc research
  • Embedded research earlier into strategic decision cycles

This work reframed research as infrastructure for better decisions.

Focus areas: Research ops · Executive dashboards · Strategic planning · Governance
